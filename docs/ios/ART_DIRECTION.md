# Art direction

## Visual target

A premium miniature world made from soft vinyl, clay, and matte ceramic. Warm studio-like sunset lighting, gently imperfect surfaces, clean silhouettes, and comic facial acting matter more than detail density.

## Composition

- Portrait `9:16`; the playable event owns the central vertical axis.
- Main action occupies the lower `55–65%`; safe-area UI stays in upper corners.
- Background remains soft and low-contrast. Midground gives depth through four to six subtle parallax layers.
- A successful save forms one strong beam diagonal. Collapse breaks the tower into separate readable arcs.

## Palette

- Sky: peach, apricot, coral, and dusty rose.
- Distance: mauve cliffs, cream haze, muted cyan atmosphere.
- Characters: olive pear, sky-blue cube, coral bird, violet rabbit, translucent cyan jelly king with a gold crown.
- UI: cocoa and powder-pink toy plates with warm cream glyphs.

## Character silhouette contract

- Pear: heavy base, narrow crown, leaf tuft.
- Cube: rounded square, tiny feet.
- Bird: egg body, small wings and crest.
- Rabbit: compact drop with two long ears.
- Jelly king: soft triangular dome, scalloped base, separate crown.

## Motion beats

- Idle breathing and eye darts.
- Ears, leaves, wings, and crown lag behind beam motion.
- Wind affects appendages and faces before full-body displacement.
- Near-save: lower face strains, middle bodies panic, upper body briefly separates, then rebounds.
- Collapse: five distinct arcs, crown ejection, restrained dust and impact stars, then readable dazed faces.

## Objective visual acceptance

- All five creatures are identifiable from silhouette alone.
- The eye reads tower, beam, then UI.
- Faces remain legible at iPhone gameplay scale.
- Background never competes in sharpness or saturation.
- Beam, characters, and buttons share the same material and light language.
- A screenshot from start, play, near-save, collapse, and results looks like a promotional frame rather than a technical mockup.
